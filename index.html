About Me

I am an Indian national currently residing in Japan. I am pursuing my master's degree in the Intelligent Mechanical Systems program at the University of Tsukuba, while also working as a research assistant at the Mobility Services Research Team in the Digital Architecture Research Center of the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST), Japan.

I completed my bachelor's degree from the Okutomi-Tanaka Lab in the Department of Systems and Control Engineering at the Tokyo Institute of Technology, under the guidance of Professor Masatoshi Okutomi. During my undergraduate studies, I collaborated with Olympus Corp on a research project focused on "CNN-Based Parts Classification for Camera Localization in Pipe Network".

My primary research interests include computer vision and autonomous driving. I am working on developing a monitoring support system for autonomous bus services, leveraging computer vision technology to ensure passenger safety. My studies have allowed me to acquire expertise in machine learning, information processing, and other essential tools in the field of intelligent mechanical systems.

As I look to the future, I aim to enhance my skills and expertise in AI and data science to contribute to the advancement of these fields and support global teams using my multilingual abilities. My strong curiosity and love for learning new things drive me to continually adapt to rapidly evolving technologies, provide customer-oriented solutions, and tackle challenges to improve my problem-solving abilities and technical prowess as an engineer.

In my leisure time, I enjoy photography and editing, though I am still an amateur in these areas. I am also actively exploring video making and cinematography to expand my creative skills. As a passionate follower of Cricket and Chess, I stay updated on the latest developments and events in these sports.
